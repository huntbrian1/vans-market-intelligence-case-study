# Vans Market Expansion Strategy (2021)

A market-intelligence and marketing-strategy case study evaluating how Vans could expand beyond its skateboarding, surfing, and BMX roots without weakening the authenticity, accessible pricing, and community relationships behind its customer loyalty.

> **Historical scope:** This analysis reflects the original 2021 research period. Market facts, competitive conditions, and recommendations should be interpreted in that context.

## Project overview

The project combined secondary market research, customer segmentation, competitive analysis, positioning, marketing-mix development, implementation planning, and performance controls. The portfolio edition opens with a five-page executive market-intelligence brief and then presents a tightened narrative adaptation of the original marketing plan.

**Project lead:** Brian Hunt  
**Original context:** Undergraduate marketing plan project  
**Research period:** 2021

## Business question

How could Vans enter adjacent growth markets while protecting the cultural specificity and customer loyalty that made the brand valuable?

## Analysis included

- Internal and external market assessment
- SWOT and competitor analysis
- Customer segmentation and launch-journey mapping
- Brand positioning and value-proposition alignment
- Product, pricing, distribution, and promotional strategy
- Qualitative opportunity prioritization
- Implementation roadmap, KPIs, and investment controls

## Strategic recommendations

The analysis recommended a sequence of disciplined, evidence-based adjacencies:

1. **Validate lifting and gym demand first** using existing product behavior before developing a specialized shoe.
2. **Pilot indoor soccer** as the strongest new-product concept through a limited release and credible community partnerships.
3. **Build rock-climbing credibility before product credibility** through sponsorship, research, and community participation.
4. **Treat hiking as a longer-term portfolio opportunity** supported by potential collaboration with Timberland and The North Face.

## Core takeaway

Vans' strongest path was not maximum category reach. It was disciplined adjacency: identify communities whose needs genuinely fit the brand, contribute to those communities, validate demand, and scale only when customer and financial evidence aligned.
